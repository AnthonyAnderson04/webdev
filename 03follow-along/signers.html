Who Were the signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independance include

John Hancock
As President of the Continental Congress, Hancock famously signed the Declaration first with a large, bold signature, making "John Hancock" a synonym for a signature.
John Adams
A leading voice for independence, Adams was on the committee that drafted the Declaration and later became the second U.S. President.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, he was a key organizer in the revolutionary movement and participated in the Boston Tea Party.
Benjamin Franklin
The oldest signer at 70, this world-famous scientist, inventor, and diplomat served on the drafting committee and made a famous edit to Jefferson's writing.
Benjamin Rush
A physician and Founding Father from Pennsylvania, Rush was a medical and social reformer.
Robert Morris
A wealthy merchant from Pennsylvania, Morris played a crucial role in securing funding for the Revolutionary War.
Thomas Jefferson
As the primary author of the Declaration of Independence, Jefferson composed the document laying out the colonists' grievances and natural rights and later became the third U.S. President.
Richard Henry Lee
A radical member of the Virginia House of Burgesses who introduced the resolution for independence to the Continental Congress.
Benjamin Harrison
A wealthy planter from Virginia, Harrison was the father and great-grandfather of two U.S. presidents.
Roger Sherman
From Connecticut, Sherman was the only person to sign all four of the major founding documents of the United States.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Roman Catholic signer and the last surviving signer of the Declaration, he was a wealthy landowner from Maryland.
Button Gwinnett
An immigrant from England, Gwinnett was a signer from Georgia whose signature is one of the rarest and most valuable.

each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered tresonous by the British Crown.
